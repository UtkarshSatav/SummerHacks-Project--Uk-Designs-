# Ghost — The Pitch

## "The tax of being talented"

---

There's a freelancer in Pune named Rohan.

He's genuinely good at what he does — React developer, clean code, ships on time. His clients love him. The problem is finding clients in the first place.

Every Sunday evening, Rohan spends three hours scrolling Reddit. r/forhire. r/webdev. r/startups. He reads through hundreds of posts — most of them irrelevant, some of them already cold. By the time he finds a good one, writes a personalised proposal, checks the guy's post history to understand the context, figures out a fair price, and finally sends something — it's midnight. He's exhausted. And he still has actual client work due Monday morning.

He calls this **"the tax of being talented."**

The better you are at your craft, the more time you lose to everything that isn't your craft — prospecting, pitching, chasing, invoicing, following up, worrying about which client is going silent, wondering if you're charging too little, starting sentences with "Just checking in on..." and hating yourself for it.

Rohan is not bad at business. He's just doing business the wrong way — alone, manually, at midnight on a Sunday.

**Ghost exists to fix this.**

---

## What Ghost Does

Ghost is an AI freelance business partner. Not a chatbot. Not a CRM. A partner — one that works while you sleep.

Every 30 minutes, Ghost scans Reddit. It reads thousands of posts across subreddits matched to your niche and skills. It filters out the noise — the "for hire" posts where other freelancers are offering services, the vague posts with no real intent — and surfaces only the ones where someone is genuinely looking to pay someone like you to do something you're actually good at. Each lead is scored 0–100 for hiring intent.

When Rohan opens Ghost on Monday morning, there are 16 leads waiting for him. Pre-scored. Pre-ranked. The top one: a funded startup founder in Mumbai, looking for a React developer for a fintech dashboard. Score: 89. Urgency: high. Budget signal: mentioned. Ghost already wrote him an opener.

He clicks **Draft Proposal**. Ghost reads the post, reads Rohan's profile, and writes a 180-word proposal in his voice — personalised to the specific post, no "[Your Name]" placeholders, no generic nonsense. It also ran pricing analysis in parallel: ₹75,000 for this scope, enterprise client, senior-level work. The price is embedded in the proposal.

Total time from "open app" to "proposal sent": 4 minutes.

That used to take Rohan 90.

## The Three Problems Ghost Solves

### 1. Finding clients is a full-time job by itself

The average freelancer spends 15–20 hours a month on business development — and most of that time is wasted on dead-end leads. Ghost compresses that to under 30 minutes a week by doing the scanning, scoring, and qualifying automatically. You only see leads that pass the bar.

### 2. Writing proposals is emotionally draining

There's a specific kind of dread that comes with a blank proposal document. You know you need to say something real, something that doesn't sound like every other proposal the client received. You stare at the post for ten minutes. You write three sentences. You delete two of them.

Ghost doesn't have that dread. It just writes. And because it knows your niche, your skills, your experience level, and the specific post — it writes something that actually sounds like a person who has read the thing and has something useful to say about it.

### 3. Client relationships decay silently

Priya is a UX designer in Bangalore. She's great at the work. What she's not great at is remembering to check in with clients between milestones. Not because she doesn't care — because she has four active projects and she's the only person running all of them.

One of her clients went quiet three weeks ago. She noticed, but she was busy. When she finally reached out, he'd already hired someone else. He didn't ghost her out of rudeness — he just assumed she wasn't paying attention.

Ghost flags clients before this happens. It calculates a health score based on last contact date, project status, payment status, and completion percentage. When a relationship is trending toward "at risk", Ghost surfaces it in the daily brief — and writes the re-engagement message for her.

She still has to send it. But she doesn't have to notice the problem, figure out the right thing to say, and psych herself up to send it — all while running four other projects. Ghost handles the noticing and the drafting. She handles the sending.

## The Daily Brief

Every morning, Ghost writes a 5-item prioritised action plan based on your live pipeline data.

Not generic. Not "check your email." Specific:

- *"URGENT — Respond to the Stripe checkout post on r/webdev — Score 94, posted 3 hours ago, budget signal strong."*
- *"TODAY — Follow up on the Mumbai fintech proposal — 4 days with no reply, one well-timed message can revive this."*
- *"TODAY — Check in with Priya's client — hasn't been contacted in 11 days, project at 60%, payment due next week."*

It reads like a briefing from someone who has been watching your business while you slept. Because it has been.
